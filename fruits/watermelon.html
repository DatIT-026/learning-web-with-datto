Watermelon

About Watermelon

Watermelon is a large, juicy fruit known for its sweet, refreshing taste and high water content. It is typically green on the outside with a bright red or pink interior filled with black seeds. Watermelon is popular in summer as a hydrating snack.

Nutrition Facts

One cup (about 152 grams) of diced watermelon contains approximately:

  • Calories: 46
  • Protein: 0.9 grams
  • Carbohydrates: 12 grams
  • Fiber: 0.6 grams
  • Sugar: 9.4 grams (natural)
  • Fat: 0.2 grams
  • Vitamin C: 12.5 mg (21% daily value)
  • Vitamin A: 865 IU (17% daily value)
  • Potassium: 170 mg
  • Magnesium: 15 mg

Health Benefits

  • High water content helps keep you hydrated, especially in hot weather.
  • Rich in antioxidants like lycopene, which may reduce the risk of certain cancers and support heart health.
  • Contains vitamins A and C that support immune function and skin health.
  • May help reduce inflammation and muscle soreness after exercise.
  • Low in calories and fat, making it a great healthy snack option.

How to Enjoy Watermelon

  • Eat fresh slices or cubes as a refreshing snack.
  • Add to fruit salads or smoothies for extra sweetness and hydration.
  • Make watermelon juice or sorbet for a cooling treat.
  • Use in salads with feta cheese and mint for a savory twist.

Fun Fact

Watermelon is over 90% water, making it one of the most hydrating fruits you can eat. It also belongs to the same family as cucumbers, pumpkins, and squash.
